Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

Effective date: 01/07/2025

Company: The Prod Lab Ltd. (“we”, “us”, “our”)

Registered in Scotland, Company No: SC825921

1. Purpose of this policy

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose and safeguard personal data when you visit our website, register as a candidate, or engage our services as a client. We process personal data in accordance with the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

2. Who is the data controller?

The Prod Lab Ltd. is the data controller for personal data processed in connection with our recruitment and related services. For any privacy enquiries, contact:

  • Email: hello@theprodlab.com

3. What data we collect

We may collect and process the following categories of personal data:

  • Candidate data: name, contact details, location, work eligibility, LinkedIn/profile links, CV/resumé (including employment history, education, skills, certifications), role/seniority preferences, salary expectations, availability/notice period, interview notes and scorecards, communications with us.
  • Client data: company details, contact name and role, email, phone, job specifications, fee agreements, billing details and correspondence.
  • Website & usage data: IP address, device/browser information, and analytics or cookies as described in our Cookie Notice.
  • Special category data: We do not intentionally collect sensitive data. If you provide such data (e.g., health or diversity information) we will only process it with your explicit consent or where legally permitted.

4. How we collect data

  • Directly from you (forms, emails, calls, interviews, events).
  • From publicly available sources (e.g., LinkedIn, company websites) where appropriate.
  • From referrals or third‑party job boards/ATS tools where you have made your data available for recruitment purposes.

5. Why we use your data (lawful bases)

We process personal data for the following purposes and lawful bases:

  • Providing recruitment and advisory services to candidates and clients (Article 6(1)(b) contract; 6(1)(f) legitimate interests in running a recruitment business).
  • Candidate assessment & matching (screening calls, scorecards, references with permission) (6(1)(b), 6(1)(f)).
  • Client relationship management, proposals and billing (6(1)(b), 6(1)(f)).
  • Communications and updates you request or opt into (6(1)(a) consent; you may withdraw at any time).
  • Legal and compliance including record keeping and responding to lawful requests (6(1)(c)).
  • Where we rely on legitimate interests, we balance those interests against your rights; details are available on request.

6. Sharing your data

We may share personal data with:

  • Prospective employers/clients to consider you for roles — only with your prior knowledge/consent.
  • Service providers (processors) who support our business (hosting, ATS/CRM, email, analytics, resume parsing) under contract and confidentiality.
  • Professional advisers (legal/accounting) and authorities where required by law.

7. International transfers

If we transfer personal data outside the UK, we will use appropriate safeguards such as the UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) or Standard Contractual Clauses, or rely on an adequacy decision.

8. How long we keep data (retention)

  • Candidates: typically up to 24 months from last meaningful contact, so we can keep you in mind for roles, unless you ask us to delete sooner.
  • Clients & transactional records: typically up to 6 years for tax and legal purposes.
  • We may retain minimal suppression data to respect opt‑out requests.

9. Your rights

Under UK data protection law you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data and obtain a copy.
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Request erasure in certain circumstances.
  • Restrict or object to processing, including processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing.
  • Data portability for data you provided to us.
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent (this does not affect processing before withdrawal).

To exercise your rights, contact hello@theprodlab.com. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling your request.

10. Security

We use appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data (access controls, encryption in transit, least‑privilege access, staff confidentiality). No method of transmission or storage is completely secure; we cannot guarantee absolute security.

11. Cookies & analytics

We may use cookies and similar technologies for functionality and analytics. See our Cookie Notice for details and choices.

12. Children

Our services are not directed to children under 16 and we do not knowingly collect their data.

13. Complaints

If you have concerns about our use of your data, please contact us first at hello@theprodlab.com. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the UK supervisory authority:

  • Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) — ico.org.uk | Tel: 0303 123 1113

14. Updates to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The “Effective date” above will be revised and, where appropriate, we will notify you of material changes.
